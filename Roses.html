The Story of Roses

Original cultivation of European roses predates Christ and began with the genus; Gallica (gallica officinalis) which is referred to as "the apothecary's rose". In Egyptian history there are recordings of Mediterranean plants that were cultivated that include the genus R. damascena, the Damask rose. These and other varieties of the sort are found in Greece and throughout the Roman Empire during that period. At around 100 A.D. Alba roses descended from the Gallica genus, crossing with the Dog rose (R. canina) and rising in popularity throughout the Renaissance. Many Italian paintings include the R. Alba. During the period around 1500, the Centifolia Rose was produced by Dutch hybridizers. R. centifolia are known as the "Cabbage Rose". In 1696 Moss roses derived from a cross of the Damask and China roses. As European development grew, as did China roses (Rosa Chimensis) in the 18th century. The first tea rose (R. odorata) debuted shortly after. The new European class of roses was difficult to breed the cross between the China roses and Damask, yet the Bourbon roses came to be. Similar are the Hybrid Bourbons that only bloom once annually. Hybrid Perpetuals are intercrossed China, Bourbans, Portlands (which is a cross of China and Teas) and Tea roses. These began growing in the mid 1800's and developed into more than 4000 varieties. In the United States, in the early 1800's, the first hybrid rose to be bred from the Musks rose (R. moschata) crossed with the Tea roses, named the Noisette. Hybrid Perpetuals and Tea roses were crossed in 1860's creating the classification of Hybrid Teas. In the turn of the 20th century, yellow roses grew in the strain R. foetida, the new class of Pernetiana roses. But this class was short lived as it fell into the same category as Hybrid Teas in the 1930's. Miniature roses (R. chimensis minima) soared in popularity in the early 1800's in France, then disappeared and reappeared in Switzerland in the 1920's. Hybrid Musks were introduced in the 1920's which were versions of the Noisettes, crossed with Rosa multiflora ramblers. Rosa multiflora crossed with Tea and China roses created Polyanthas. A French rose that was made the same time as the Hybrid Teas. Hybrid Polyanthas are also known as Floribundas. The word translates to "flowers in abundance" as it has many blooms. In the 1950's, Grandiflora's were classified as a intermediate between Hybrid Teas and Floribundas as they're born in clusters, like Floribundas but grow tall like Hybrids. Shrub roses have always had a roll throughout history and contributed to the development of modern roses. These vary from native species to Sweetbrierbs (R. rubiginosa), Rugosas (R. rugosa), Nevada, Chestnut (R. roxbughii) Austrian Copper (R. foetida), and Kordesii roses.
